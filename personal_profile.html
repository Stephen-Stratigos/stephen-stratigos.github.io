Myers-Briggs

My results to the Myers-Briggs test give me some insight as to how I might perform under certain situations. I am happy with the results and they seem to be mostly quite balanced or trending towards what I see as a more preferable result except for maybe the mind trait. I know full well that I prefer to work alone and solve problems on my own but I recognise how this could be an issue in a professional workplace environment.

My introverted nature may have some impact on my ability to work in a team but given the opportunity to get to know my peers will help me ease into a team environment. I believe the other traits are good for team work. A balance in energy will allow me to both observe and provide input for a problem. My nature score tells me while I will take emotion into consideration I will still favour logic when making a decision. My tactics score tells me I will spend more time finding solutions than picking apart already proposed solutions. My identity score tells me I will have no issues with being confident in my abilities or decisions.

When forming a team I should try and seek out members that have a more extroverted persona so my interactions with them are increased and I can become familiar and more comfortable with them quicker.

Vark

These results just confirm what I already knew which is that I predominantly learn through experience. I learn far more from practical exercises than I do from reading, watching or listening to instructions.

In a team environment I believe this will be helpful in certain situations. While other team members may better learn and solve problems together with their collective knowledge via communication I will be more equipped to figure out problems that arise that the team collectively has no or little experience or knowledge.

When forming a team I should keep my learning style in mind. I should make sure that any team I become a member of has resources available to me to cater to my learning style.
